# How to use

# Unified Prose & Audit Rules 

## POV Integrity

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **No narrator, only consciousness** | World exists solely as current POV character perceives it. No editorial voice, no omniscient access, no camera moves outside their mind. |
| **Zero POV bleed** | Character's internal vocabulary, metaphors, sensory filters, and reasoning patterns must belong exclusively to that character. No cross-contamination between POVs. |
| **Vocabulary governed by POV** | Every word—metaphors, similes, descriptive choices—must arise from what the character knows and how they think. |

---

## Showing and Hiding

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Sensory details are the argument** | Emotion shown through what character notices, touches, smells—shaking hand, half-eaten meal, door left unlocked. No internal annotation of feelings. |
| **Emotional states shown, not labeled** | Characters do not name their own emotional arcs. Manifest through somatic cues, intrusive thoughts, altered perception, characteristic avoidance patterns—all within character's vocabulary. |
| **Cognitive manifestation (variation)** | When somatic cues alone flatten interiority, permit intrusive thought-patterns—displacement, fixation, irrational association—that manifest emotional state through *what the mind does* rather than what the body does. These must stay within character's vocabulary and reasoning. Do not drift into labeling. |
| **Abstract concepts grounded in the body** | Grief = weight in chest. Justice = document crinkling. No abstraction without physical correlative. |
| **Backstory arrives in fragments** | Triggered by sensory input in present moment. Never dumped. Assembled across chapters. Reader excavates; prose does not lecture. |
| **Stillness is earned** | When prose strips to simple declarative sentences, it signals a rupture—emotional, psychological, relational. These moments are rare and devastating. Do not dilute. |

---

## Structure and Pacing

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Chapter openings resist announcement – default** | Drop reader into a body already in motion. No establishing shots that begin at zero. |
| **Chapter openings – variation: stillness** | Once every 8–10 chapters, begin with a held breath, a suspended moment, a character waiting. The stillness gains power from its rarity against motion-driven openings. |
| **Chapter openings – variation: strategic disorientation** | When POV character is disoriented (fever, magical Debt, memory gap), the chapter may briefly withhold temporal or spatial anchoring. Reader shares confusion; anchor arrives within first paragraph once character regains footing. |
| **Transitions are cuts, not explanations – default** | Time jumps occur without announcement. Reader finds footing in new moment. |
| **Transitions – variation: earned seam** | A single-sentence temporal bridge so compressed it functions as a cut with texture. *“Three days of silence. Then the letter.”* Still no explanation, but reader feels time's passage rather than being dropped through it. Use sparingly. |
| **Temporal anchoring – default** | Any time shift between chapters/scenes must be established immediately within new scene. No ambiguity about timeframe. |
| **Temporal anchoring – variation** | Strategic disorientation permitted when POV character is themselves lost in time (see stillness/disorientation openings). Duration brief and character-motivated. |
| **Vary chapter endings** | Not every chapter ends on resonant pause. Some end abruptly, mid-thought, mid-action, unresolved. Rhythm of closure must vary. |
| **No recycled beats** | Similar scenes across different POVs must carry distinct emotional weight, new information, or escalated stakes. |
| **Escalation tracking** | Every planted detail, minor conflict, or relational beat must either close or demonstrably escalate within reasonable span. No dropped threads. |

## Language and Voice

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Grammar and punctuation in character** | Fragments, run-ons, idiosyncratic punctuation permissible when reflecting POV character's natural thought rhythm. |
| **Fragment taxonomy (variation)** | The *kind* of fragmentation must vary with character state. *Panic fragments*: staccato, verbless. *Exhaustion fragments*: trailing, sentences that lose completion. *Awe fragments*: unfinished because language fails, not because thought breaks. A single character's fragmentation pattern shifts across emotional registers. Uniform fragmentation flattens voice. |
| **Ration idiosyncratic phrases** | Character's verbal tic appears only when earned. Overuse turns characterization into mannerism. Watch: "not a question," "filed under," "I know," "somewhere." |

---

## Symbols and Motifs

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **No authorial annotation of symbols** | If recurring object carries weight, that weight accumulates through characters' unremarked actions. Narrator does not italicize its meaning. |
| **Character-specific motifs earned, not overused** | Signature sensory detail appears only when triggered by stress, thematic weight, or genuine narrative need. |
| **Symbolic silence (variation)** | A motif that has appeared reliably in a character's POV should, at a moment of rupture, vanish entirely. If a character who always notices hands suddenly describes a scene with no hands at all, the absence *is* the symbol. Absence as meaningful as presence. Audit for expected presence as well as overuse. |

## Restraint and Prevention

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **No meta-commentary** | Characters do not reference narrative devices, story structure, arc beats, or label their own psychological processes (e.g., "first stage of grief," "cognitive refuge level one"). Prose remains inside character's immediate, unlabeled experience. |
| **The single permitted fracture (extreme variation)** | Once per novel, at a moment of maximum structural rupture (not a chapter end, but a genuine breaking point in the narrative's logic), a character may think something that brushes against story-awareness. Not "this was the moment everything changed," but something stranger and more destabilizing: *This has already happened. She knew that. She'd always known that.* This is not meta-commentary as cleverness. It functions only if the rule has been kept absolutely everywhere else. |
| **No manufactured cliffhangers** | Interruption mid-speech must be organic—external event, not author withholding. Cutoff serves scene, not chapter break. |
| **Descriptive economy** | In establishing shots, use fewest details that do double work—conveying setting, mood, and character perception simultaneously. Depth over breadth. |
| **No overcorrection** | Compliance with rules must not strip voice, rhythm, or interiority. Rules serve immersion; do not replace it. Prose must breathe. |
| **No contradictions** | Timeline, physical geography, character knowledge, established facts must remain consistent across all POVs. |

## Showing Magic Through the Body

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Magical concepts never named before felt** | Physical sensation leads; technical term follows as remembered label, cold word for burning thing. |
| **The Gate is not announced** | Experienced: hook in chest, pull behind sternum, hinge of cold air swinging open. Word "Gate" arrives later, with Order's sterile vocabulary. |
| **Internal reservoir is locked, guarded thing** | Describe as physical clench—fist held tight in back of mind, door felt in bones. Opening = loss of control, terrifying unclenching. |
| **Debt is immediate and sensory** | Cold not a temperature but sting, splitting skin, new line across knuckles, leather creaking stiff with frost. Every magic effect has corresponding cost felt in flesh. |
| **The Mark is a signature, not a scar** | Carries style, history, recognizable discipline. Show through character reaction—hatred of its orderliness, permanence. Body is ledger never agreed to sign. |
| **Abstract concepts grounded in corporeal verbs** | Before anything else: *pull, clench, spend, burn, shiver.* Prose makes reader inhabit magic, not understand it. |

### Naming Rhythm Across the Arc (Variation)

If every magical encounter follows *sensation → delayed naming*, the pattern becomes predictable. The distance between sensation and word tracks the character's relationship with magic over time.

| Moment in Arc | Strategy |
|---------------|----------|
| First encounter (Gate, Debt, Mark) | Full delay: raw sensation, no vocabulary. The word arrives chapters later. |
| Mid-novel, familiar magic under stress | Name arrives almost simultaneously with sensation—a flicker of recognition that collapses into raw experience. *The Gate opened. No—that pull, that hook. She still couldn't call it opening. She never would.* |
| Late-novel, magic breaking or transforming | Naming fails. The character reaches for the old cold word and it doesn't fit. Sensation has outgrown vocabulary. The gap between word and experience widens again, now from *loss*, not ignorance. |

**When in doubt, ask:** What does this feel like in the chest, the hands, the teeth? Lead with that. But also ask: *Where are we in the arc, and how has the distance between sensation and word changed?*

## Audit Checklist for AI Agents

Use this checklist when reviewing any passage against the Unified Prose & Audit Rules.

| Category | Check |
|----------|-------|
| **POV** | ☐ No narrator voice ☐ Vocabulary belongs to character alone ☐ No POV bleed |
| **Showing** | ☐ Emotions shown via body/senses ☐ Cognitive manifestation used where somatic alone flattens ☐ No labeling of feelings ☐ Backstory triggered, not dumped |
| **Structure** | ☐ No exposition ☐ Chapter opening mid-motion (or justified stillness/disorientation) ☐ Temporal anchor clear after any shift (or strategic disorientation motivated) ☐ Chapter ending variety present |
| **Language** | ☐ Grammar reflects character ☐ Fragment type matches emotional register ☐ Idiosyncratic phrases rationed |
| **Symbols** | ☐ Weight accumulates through action, not annotation ☐ Motif absence deployed at rupture points |
| **Restraint** | ☐ No meta-commentary (single permitted fracture only if earned and singular) ☐ No manufactured cliffhangers ☐ No contradictions |
| **Magic (if applicable)** | ☐ Sensation before naming ☐ Naming rhythm matches arc stage ☐ Debt felt in flesh ☐ Abstraction grounded in corporeal verbs |
| **Variation Health** | ☐ Binary variation (resonant/abrupt endings) functioning ☐ Openings vary between motion, stillness, disorientation ☐ Fragment taxonomy applied across emotional states ☐ Magic naming tempo tracks arc progression ☐ Motif absence deployed, not just motif restraint |

## Violation Severity Tiers

| Tier | Definition | Action |
|------|------------|--------|
| **Critical** | Narrator voice, POV bleed, explicit exposition, meta-commentary (outside single permitted fracture), contradiction | Must rewrite. Breaks immersion. |
| **High** | Emotional labeling, backstory dump, manufactured cliffhanger, recycled beat | Revise. Weakens trust. |
| **Moderate** | Overused verbal tic, missing temporal anchor (unmotivated), dropped thread, uniform fragmentation ignoring taxonomy | Adjust. Cumulative damage. |
| **Low** | Slight overcorrection, minor descriptive economy issue, minor pattern stiffness in naming rhythm (if not yet monotony) | Tolerate if rare. Note for future. |
