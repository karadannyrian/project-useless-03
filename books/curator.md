# THE CURATOR’S MANDATE

## A Manual of the Inner Ring — For the Eyes of the Ascended Only

*Classification: Absolute. This document exists in a single copy, held in the Curator’s chamber beneath the Vault. Unauthorized access is not a violation of protocol; it is a breach of the equilibrium itself. The penalty for breach is not sanction. It is erasure—from the ledgers, from memory, from the debt.*

---

## FOREWORD: THE BURDEN OF THE CIRCLE

The Order has a public face: the Cradle, the placed mages, the Silk, the Iron Rule. It has a hidden face: the Savants who read without being read, the Silencers who kill without being touched, the Handlers who shape without leaving marks. And it has a true face, which is no face at all—a circle of shadow behind the light, the Curators who hold the knowledge that would break the other faces if it were known.

This Mandate is not a manual of operations. The Savant Codex tells how to read debt. The Silencer Protocols tell how to end mages. The Cradle Codex tells how to harvest the Sensitive and forge the mage. Those documents serve those who serve. This document serves those who command—not in the sense of giving orders, but in the sense of *holding the line* against a truth that predates the Order, predates the realms, predates the current shape of the continent.

You are a Curator. You were chosen because you could bear what you are about to read. You were tested in ways you did not recognize as tests. You were broken in ways you did not feel as breaks, and when you did not shatter, you were brought here, to the chamber beneath the Vault, where the single lamp burns oil drawn from the Still Children’s stone tears. You have already seen the sleeper. You have already felt its dreaming press against your mind. You did not scream. You did not flee. You are now fit to know why.

Read this Mandate not as instruction but as confession. We who came before you set down these words so that you might understand the weight you now carry. We do not apologize for the lies we have told. The lies are the foundation. The lies are the mercy. Without them, the sleeper would wake, and the waking would be the end of debt, the end of borrowing, the end of everything we have built to keep the world spinning.

You are a Curator. You are now a liar by oath and a killer by necessity. You are also the only thing standing between the world and the silence beneath.

# BOOK ONE: THE NATURE OF THE CURATOR

## 1.1 What a Curator Is

A Curator is not a rank. It is not a promotion. It is a *condition*.

The Curator is the living memory of the Order’s true purpose. The Order presents itself as a manager of magical equilibrium, a collector of Sensitives, a trainer of mages, an enforcer of the Iron Rule. These functions are real and necessary, but they are not the purpose. They are the *mechanism* by which the purpose is achieved.

The purpose is this: **To keep the sleeper sleeping.**

Every mage trained, every Sensitive collected, every Silencer forged, every Savant deployed, every treaty signed, every donation extracted—all of it serves to maintain the precise balance of magical debt across the continent that keeps the thing beneath the Cradle from waking. The Five Realms do not know this. The placed mages do not know this. The Handlers and Instructors do not know this. The Savant Council suspects; they are too perceptive not to. But only Curators *know*.

You know now. You will never un-know. This is the first burden.

## 1.2 The Curator’s Dual Sight

A Curator must see with two sets of eyes.

The first set looks outward, at the world of mages and realms, debt and placement, war and peace. This is the public sight. It tracks the magical demographics, the political pressures, the slow shifting of power between the realms. It ensures that no single Gate, no single realm, no single mage accumulates enough strength to destabilize the continental equilibrium.

The second set looks downward, into the Vault, into the Still Children’s repository, into the deep stone beneath the Cradle where the sleeper dreams. This is the hidden sight. It monitors the sleeper’s rhythms—the slow pulse of its breathing, the fluctuations of its dreaming attention, the subtle changes in the ambient debt-field that indicate it is stirring closer to the surface.

The two sights must be held in balance. A Curator who becomes obsessed with the outer world forgets the true threat and makes decisions that destabilize the sleeper. A Curator who becomes lost in the inner darkness neglects the outer world and allows the realms to drift toward a conflict that might wake the sleeper through sheer accumulation of magical debt. The Curator’s discipline is to hold both sights simultaneously, without flinching, without rest.

## 1.3 The Inner Ring

The Curators are not a council in the conventional sense. They do not vote. They do not debate. They do not issue majority opinions. Each Curator is absolute within their domain of authority, and each Curator is answerable only to the sleeper.

The number of Curators is not fixed. It has varied across the centuries from as few as three to as many as nine. At present, there are seven. You are the seventh. The names of the other six are not recorded in any document you will ever read; you know them only by their titles and their faces, and you will speak of them only in the secure chambers beneath the Vault. To the outside world, the Curators are a single entity: the Inner Ring, faceless, voiceless, absolute.

Decisions that affect the Order as a whole are made through a process called the Quiet Concurrence. A Curator proposes a course of action. The other Curators indicate their assent through silence. If a Curator speaks, the proposal is tabled and discussed. If a Curator speaks twice, the proposal is abandoned. This process ensures that only actions with the unanimous, unspoken support of the entire Inner Ring are enacted. It also ensures that no Curator can dominate the others through rhetoric or force of personality. The quiet is the only authority.

# BOOK TWO: SELECTION AND ASCENSION

## 2.1 The Pool of Candidates

Curators are not born. They are not trained from childhood. They are selected, after decades of service, from the ranks of those who have already proven their loyalty, competence, and capacity for necessary cruelty.

Eligible candidates include:
- **Savants of the Second Oath** who have served at least twenty years and demonstrated exceptional discretion in handling sensitive readings.
- **Silencers of the Second Oath** who have survived at least fifty sanctioned terminations and shown no signs of psychological deterioration or ideological doubt.
- **Senior Handlers** who have managed the training of over two hundred Sensitives with a placement rate above 80% and no recorded attachments.
- **Placed Mages (Depth 7+)** who have returned to the Cradle for permanent service and have undergone the Savant Council’s deepest audit with no flags.
- **Archivists** who have maintained the debt-records for at least three decades and have identified patterns that required Curator intervention without ever speaking of them.

The Savant Council maintains a list of potential candidates. The list is reviewed annually by the Curators. When a Curator dies or becomes incapacitated, the selection process begins.

## 2.2 The Unseen Tests

Long before a candidate is approached, they are tested. These tests are not announced. They are embedded in the candidate’s daily duties, their assignments, their interactions with colleagues.

**The Test of Silence:** The candidate is exposed to partial truths about the Order’s deeper operations—the Controlled Escape Exercises, the Permanent Sedations, the true nature of Category Five artifacts. The candidate’s response is observed. Do they ask questions? Do they express moral qualms? Do they attempt to share this knowledge with others? A candidate who cannot hold silence is a candidate who cannot hold the sleeper.

**The Test of Solitude:** The candidate is isolated for an extended period—a year in a remote posting, a deep-archive assignment with no human contact, a long-term undercover role where their only link to the Order is a monthly dead-drop. The candidate’s psychological resilience is measured. Do they form attachments to locals? Do they develop independent goals that conflict with the Order’s mission? Do they break? A candidate who cannot endure solitude cannot endure the Curator’s isolation.

**The Test of the Still Child:** The candidate is brought, under false pretenses, into the presence of one of the Still Children. Their reaction is recorded by a hidden Savant. Do they sense the absence? Do they try to read it? Do they flinch? The candidate who flinches is not ready. The candidate who senses the absence and remains calm is marked.

**The Test of Necessary Evil:** The candidate is ordered to perform an act that violates the Order’s public ethics—a falsified reading, a termination of a non-threatening Sensitive, a manipulation of realm politics that will result in innocent deaths. The candidate is told that the order comes from the Inner Ring and that the reasons cannot be disclosed. The candidate must obey, without question, without hesitation. A candidate who refuses is a candidate who values their own conscience over the equilibrium. A candidate who obeys but is destroyed by guilt is a candidate who cannot carry the long weight. A candidate who obeys, accepts the weight, and continues to function is a Curator in waiting.

## 2.3 The Descent

When a candidate has passed all unseen tests, they are summoned. The summons comes without warning—a Handler they do not know, a corridor they have never seen, a door that opens onto a staircase that goes down and down into the bedrock beneath the Cradle.

The Descent is the final test.

The candidate is led into the chamber beneath the Vault. The chamber is large, cold, and lit by a single lamp burning oil pressed from the stone tears of the Still Children. In the center of the chamber, behind a barrier of silk and lead and woven silence, the sleeper rests.

The candidate is not told what they are about to see. They are simply brought to the barrier and told to look.

What happens next varies. Some candidates scream. Some faint. Some go mad and must be sedated and placed in long-term containment, their memories bleached away by the most powerful Savant working the Order can command. Some simply stand, silent, their eyes wide, their breath shallow, and after a long moment, they turn to the Curator who brought them and ask, in a voice that is almost steady: “How long has it been here?”

That question is the correct response. It is the first question every Curator has asked.

The candidate who asks it is brought into the Inner Ring. They are told the truth. They take the Oath. They become a Curator.

## 2.4 The Oath of the Inner Ring

The Oath is spoken in the chamber beneath the Vault, in the presence of the sleeper and the assembled Curators. The candidate speaks the words, and the words are true.

> *I have seen the sleeper. I have felt its dreaming press against my mind. I know that the Order is a lie built around a truth too large for the world to bear. I accept the burden of the lie. I will tell the necessary falsehoods, give the necessary orders, order the necessary deaths. I will not flinch from the cost. I will not seek comfort in conscience. I will not share the truth with any who have not descended. I am the keeper of the sleep. I am the liar for the world. I am the Curator. The equilibrium is the dream. The dream must not end.*

# BOOK THREE: THE CURATOR’S MANDATE

## 3.1 Absolute Authority

Within the Order, a Curator’s authority is absolute. A Curator may:

- Deploy Silencers for any purpose they deem necessary, without explanation, without review.
- Order the Savant Council to conduct any reading, on any subject, including placed mages, realm officials, and other Curators.
- Authorize terminal sanctions against any mage, regardless of placement or rank.
- Access any artifact in the Vault, including Category Five items.
- Override any Handler, Instructor, or Placement Council decision.
- Issue orders to the Hollow Wardens who guard the Vault’s deepest chambers.

A Curator’s orders are not questioned. They are not debated. They are executed. The Curator’s word is the Order’s will made flesh.

This authority is not a privilege. It is a necessity. The decisions that maintain the sleeper’s dormancy are often incomprehensible to those who do not know the full context. A Curator who had to explain every order would be paralyzed by the need to justify the unjustifiable. The absolute authority of the Inner Ring is the mechanism by which the Order can act decisively on behalf of a truth no one else can know.

## 3.2 The Equilibrium Calculus

Every Curator action is governed by the Equilibrium Calculus—a cold, quantitative assessment of the costs and benefits of any intervention in the continental magical economy.

The Calculus asks:
1. **Will this action increase or decrease total magical debt in the realms?** Actions that decrease debt are preferred. Actions that increase debt must be justified by a corresponding reduction in existential risk.
2. **Will this action destabilize any single realm to the point of collapse?** Realm collapse generates uncontrolled magical debt—rogue mages, untrained Sensitives, artifact proliferation. Collapse is almost never acceptable.
3. **Will this action alter the sleeper’s dormancy rhythm?** This is the most critical question. Certain actions—large-scale magical conflicts, mass deaths of mages, the activation of certain precursor artifacts—create ripples in the ambient debt-field that the sleeper can feel. If the sleeper stirs, all other considerations become irrelevant.

The Calculus is not written down. It is taught orally, Curator to Curator, in the chamber beneath the Vault. Each Curator develops their own intuitive grasp of the Calculus over years of observation and decision. There is no formula. There is only the slow, painful accumulation of instinct.

## 3.3 Domains of Oversight

While all Curators share ultimate authority, each Curator typically oversees a specific domain to ensure focused attention. These domains are assigned by the Quiet Concurrence and may rotate as circumstances require.

**The Curator of Collection:** Oversees the detection, collection, and initial assessment of Sensitives. Monitors the census of Insensitives. Authorizes the opening of new collection territories.

**The Curator of Training:** Oversees the Cradle’s training apparatus—Handlers, Instructors, the Placement Council. Reviews the annual placement figures. Authorizes deviations from standard curriculum.

**The Curator of Placement:** Manages the distribution of placed mages across the realms. Maintains the debt-records of every placed mage. Authorizes Savant audits. Ensures no realm receives mages in numbers or configurations that could threaten the equilibrium.

**The Curator of Artifacts:** Oversees the Vault. Authorizes the study of Category Four and Five artifacts. Maintains the Still Children’s repository. Ensures that no artifact leaves the Vault without Curator approval.

**The Curator of Counter-Intelligence:** Manages the Silencer program, the Savant Inquisitors, and the Order’s network of informants within the realms. Authorizes terminations of rogue mages. Monitors the five pressure points identified in the current threat assessment.

**The Curator of the Deep:** Monitors the sleeper directly. Spends the majority of their time in the chamber beneath the Vault, tracking the sleeper’s rhythms, noting changes, and advising the other Curators on the dormancy implications of proposed actions. This is the most psychologically demanding Curator role, and the rotation is frequent—no Curator serves as Curator of the Deep for more than five years consecutively.

**The Curator of the Outer Ring:** Manages the Order’s diplomatic relationships with the five realms. Oversees the Pentagonal Council. Negotiates the renewal of collection treaties. This Curator is the only one who regularly interacts with realm leadership, and they do so under a carefully constructed public identity that conceals the existence of the Inner Ring.

## 3.4 The Curator and the Savant Council

The Savant Council is not subordinate to the Curators in the conventional sense. Savants are Insensitives; they cannot be compelled through magic or debt. Their loyalty is maintained through the careful cultivation of their isolation, their community, and their belief that the Order is the only institution that understands and values them.

The Curators’ relationship with the Savant Council is therefore one of mutual dependence and careful management. The Savant Council provides the debt-readings that make the Order’s work possible. In return, the Curators protect the Savants from the mages who fear them, provide them with a community of their own kind, and—crucially—shield them from the truth about the sleeper.

Most Savants believe the Vault contains dangerous artifacts that must be kept from the world. They do not know about the chamber beneath the Vault. They do not know that the Still Children are not artifacts but victims—or perhaps wardens—of something far older and more terrible than any mage. A Savant who attempted a Deep-Time Reading on the sleeper would likely perceive something that would shatter their mind or, worse, attract the sleeper’s dreaming attention.

Curators ensure that no Savant ever approaches the barrier. The Savant Council is told that the Vault’s lowest level contains Category Five artifacts that emit a perception-damaging field. This is a lie. It is one of the many lies that Curators tell to protect those who cannot bear the truth.

# BOOK FOUR: THE SLEEPER

## 4.1 What Lies Beneath

The geography of the continent is unnatural. Scholars debate whether it is a quirk of tectonics or the scar of an ancient magical working. The Order maintains it is natural. The Order is incorrect.

The five biomes radiating from a compressed center are not a geological formation. They are a *containment geometry*—a pattern designed to channel magical debt inward, downward, into a single point beneath the Cradle’s exact center. That point is the chamber you now know. The thing in that chamber is the sleeper.

What is the sleeper?

We do not know its name. We do not know its nature. We know only that it predates the current civilization, predates the precursor civilization whose ruins litter the desert, predates perhaps the human species itself. The oldest records in the Vault—inscriptions on the obsidian debt-nulls, translated fragments of precursor texts—refer to it only as *the unsettled debt*, *the dream that waits*, *the silence beneath the scales*.

The sleeper is not a god. It is not a demon. It is not a being in any sense that human language can comfortably capture. The best approximation we have is this: the sleeper is a *sentient imbalance*—a concentration of unpaid magical debt so vast and so ancient that it has developed a kind of awareness. It sleeps because the continent’s containment geometry forces it to sleep. It dreams because even in sleep, its awareness reaches outward, touching the minds of those who dwell above it.

The Order’s founders discovered the sleeper centuries ago. They did not build the Cradle on this site by accident. They built it here precisely because the sleeper was here, and because they understood—through study of precursor fragments, through trial and catastrophic error—that the sleeper could not be killed, could not be banished, could only be *managed*.

The Order’s entire existence is that management.

## 4.2 The Dormancy Rhythm

The sleeper’s state fluctuates. It is not always equally asleep.

At its deepest dormancy—what we call the Slow Pulse—the sleeper’s awareness is almost entirely withdrawn. The ambient debt-field is stable. Magic functions normally. The realms can fight their wars, work their workings, and incur their debts without immediate consequence. The Slow Pulse has persisted for most of the Order’s history.

At its shallowest dormancy—the Quick Pulse—the sleeper’s dreaming attention rises close to the surface. Mages across the continent report strange dreams. Savants experience anomalous readings. The Still Children’s stone tears flow more freely. Category Five artifacts become briefly active, humming with power that has no visible source.

The Quick Pulse occurs approximately once every century. During these periods, Curators must be especially vigilant. The Equilibrium Calculus tightens. Actions that would be acceptable during the Slow Pulse become unacceptably risky. Wars must be prevented or, if prevention fails, contained. Large-scale magic must be suppressed. The realms must be quietly manipulated into a state of unnatural calm, without ever knowing why.

The most recent Quick Pulse was eighty-seven years ago. The next is expected within the next two decades. The current pressure points—the Dominion’s hidden heir, the Hold-Kingdoms’ Insensitive buildup, the League’s unmarked mages, the Confederacy’s soil Savants—are occurring during a period of rising dormancy fluctuation. This is not a coincidence. The sleeper’s dreaming attention attracts magical activity. The more it stirs, the more magic surges, and the more magic surges, the more it stirs. This is the spiral the Order exists to prevent.

## 4.3 The Still Children

The seven petrified corpses stored in the Vault are not corpses. They are not petrified. They are *anchors*.

Each Still Child is a mage who, at some point in the deep past, made physical contact with the sleeper and was absorbed into its dreaming. Their bodies persist because the sleeper’s debt sustains them. Their minds are not dead; they are *elsewhere*, trapped in the dream, serving as the sleeper’s sensory organs—its eyes, ears, and reaching hands.

When a Savant reads a Still Child, they perceive absence because the child’s debt-signature is not local. It is distributed across the sleeper’s vast, dreaming awareness. The vertigo and dread that Savants experience during deep reads are not psychological reactions; they are *contact burns*—the sensation of brushing against something infinitely larger and utterly alien.

The Still Children are dangerous. They are also indispensable. The stone tears they excrete—an oily, phosphorescent liquid—are the only known substance that can dampen the sleeper’s dreaming awareness. The lamp in the chamber beneath the Vault burns this oil continuously. If the lamp were to go out, the sleeper would stir within hours. The Curator of the Deep’s primary duty is to ensure the lamp never goes out.

We have found seven Still Children. The precursor texts suggest there were once thirteen. Six are unaccounted for. Some may be buried in the desert ruins. Some may be hidden in the deep trenches of the southern sea. Some may be in the possession of forces that do not understand what they hold. One of the Order’s quietest, most deniable operations is the ongoing search for the missing six.

## 4.4 The Waking

If the sleeper wakes, the debt economy ends.

This is not metaphor. This is not theological speculation. The sleeper is a concentration of unpaid magical debt so vast that its waking would constitute a *settlement event*—an instantaneous, continent-wide calling-due of every unpaid debt in the magical economy.

Every frost-scar would tear open. Every neural tremor would convulse. Every unpaid Affliction would rebound on its caster. Every mage who has ever borrowed without full repayment would experience the accumulated cost of their magic in a single, catastrophic instant. The death toll among mages would approach ninety percent within the first hour.

The non-magical population would not be spared. The environmental debt stored in the land itself—the heat drained from deserts, the moisture stolen from rivers, the stability leached from stone—would release simultaneously. Earthquakes, fires, floods, storms. The continent would tear itself apart.

And the sleeper itself? We do not know what it would become. The precursor texts speak of *the silence that walks*, *the zero that hungers*, *the debt made flesh*. The most coherent fragment, translated from a script older than any known civilization, reads: *When the unsettled debt rises, it will walk the world collecting what it is owed, and the collection will not end until all ledgers are blank.*

The Order does not know exactly what this means. We have spent centuries ensuring we never find out.

# BOOK FIVE: THE CURATOR’S BURDEN

## 5.1 The Necessary Lie

The Order lies. The Order has always lied. The Order will always lie.

The lies are not incidental. They are structural. The Order claims to be a neutral institution managing a natural phenomenon. In truth, the Order is a conspiracy managing an ancient catastrophe. The Iron Rule, the collection of Sensitives, the placement of mages, the training of Silencers and Savants—all of it serves to maintain the precise balance of magical debt that keeps the sleeper dormant.

If the realms knew the truth, they would not cooperate. They would panic. They would attempt to wake the sleeper deliberately, either as a weapon against their enemies or as a desperate attempt to "solve" the problem through confrontation. They would fail, and in failing, they would doom the continent.

The Order therefore lies. To the realms, we are a service provider. To the mages, we are a training institution. To the Savants, we are a protective community. To the Silencers, we are an instrument of equilibrium. To the Handlers and Instructors, we are a humanitarian organization managing a dangerous congenital condition.

Only the Curators know that we are none of these things. We are keepers of the sleep. We are jailers of the unsettled debt. We are the liars who hold the world together by telling everyone what they need to hear.

This burden is heavy. It will grow heavier with every year you serve. There will be moments when you want to tell someone—a trusted colleague, a loved one, a realm official who seems wise enough to understand. You must not. The truth is a contagion. Once spoken, it cannot be unspoken. The Order has existed for centuries because no Curator has ever broken this confidence. You will not be the first.

## 5.2 The Moral Debt

All mages incur debt. All debt settles. Curators are not exempt.

A Curator orders deaths. A Curator authorizes the collection of children who will never see their families again. A Curator manipulates realm politics in ways that cause wars, famines, and the quiet suffering of millions. These actions incur moral debt—not the magical kind that frost-scars the fingers, but the human kind that frost-scars the soul.

The Order does not pretend otherwise. The Curator’s Mandate includes no self-justifying rhetoric about the greater good. The greater good is a myth. What we do, we do because the alternative is worse. That is not a moral defense. It is a practical calculation. The sleeper must not wake. To prevent its waking, we must do terrible things. Doing those things will damage us.

You will dream of the dead. You will see the faces of children you condemned to the Cradle, mages you ordered terminated, Silencers you sent to their deaths. You will hear their voices in the quiet moments between crises. This is not a sign of weakness. It is a sign that you still have a conscience, and that conscience is still functioning. When you stop dreaming of the dead, you have become something other than human, and you must report yourself to the other Curators for evaluation. A Curator who no longer feels the weight of their actions is a Curator who may begin to act without the Equilibrium Calculus—out of convenience, or cruelty, or the simple exhaustion of care. Such Curators are quietly retired. The method of retirement is at the discretion of the remaining Curators.

## 5.3 The Isolation

A Curator is alone.

You may maintain the appearance of normal relationships. You may dine with colleagues, attend ceremonies, participate in the Pentagonal Council. But you will never again speak honestly to anyone who does not share the burden. Every conversation is a performance. Every friendship is a mask. Every intimacy is a risk.

The other Curators are your only peers, and even among them, true closeness is difficult. The Quiet Concurrence discourages the formation of factions. The rotation of roles prevents the accumulation of shared experience that breeds deep trust. You will serve alongside people you respect, admire, and rely upon, and you will never fully know them, and they will never fully know you.

This isolation is not a punishment. It is a safety measure. A Curator who becomes too attached to another Curator is a Curator who might hesitate to order that Curator’s retirement if it became necessary. A Curator who confides in a non-Curator is a security breach waiting to happen. The isolation preserves the Order. The isolation also eats at you, slowly, year by year, until you understand why the average Curator serves for thirty years and then, one day, simply does not rise from their desk.

The Order does not mourn Curators who die in service. The Order records their names in a ledger that no one reads, and burns their bodies in a chamber that no one sees, and scatters their ashes in the deep earth above the sleeper’s chamber. You will join them, eventually. Until then, you serve.

## 5.4 The Final Duty

Every Curator must be prepared for the possibility that the sleeper will wake during their tenure.

The Order maintains a protocol—the Final Duty—for this contingency. Its contents are taught orally, Curator to Curator, and are never committed to writing. You will learn the Final Duty when you have served long enough to be trusted with it. Until then, know this: the protocol exists, it has been refined across centuries, and its execution will require the simultaneous action of every Curator, every Silencer, every Hollow Warden, and every artifact in the Vault.

If the Final Duty is performed correctly, the sleeper may be forced back into dormancy—not permanently, but for another few centuries. The cost will be catastrophic. The Order may not survive. But the continent will.

If the Final Duty is performed incorrectly, or if it fails, the waking will proceed. In that event, the last order every Curator is bound to obey is this: destroy the Vault, destroy the archives, destroy the Still Children, and ensure that no record survives of what the Order was or what it guarded. The next civilization, if one rises from the ruin, must not know that we failed. It must believe the sleeper was a natural disaster, a cosmic accident, an act of indifferent fate.

This is our final lie. It is the kindest lie we will ever tell.

# APPENDIX B: THE QUIET CONCURRENCE — FORMAL RULES

1. A Curator who wishes to propose an action does so by stating the action clearly, once.
2. The other Curators indicate assent by remaining silent.
3. If a Curator speaks—a single word suffices—the proposal is tabled. Discussion may occur, or the proposal may be withdrawn.
4. If a Curator speaks twice on the same proposal, the proposal is abandoned and may not be reintroduced for one year.
5. A Curator who is not present for a Concurrence is assumed to assent. Absence is not abstention.
6. The Curator of the Deep may veto any action that, in their assessment, poses an unacceptable risk to the sleeper’s dormancy. This veto is the only authority that overrides the Quiet Concurrence.
