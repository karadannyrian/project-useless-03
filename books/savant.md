## FOREWORD: THE EYE THAT SEES THE BALANCE

The Order's authority rests on a single, irreducible claim: we know the truth of magic. We know what borrowing costs. We know what debt does to flesh. We know the ledger of every mage who has ever passed through our halls, and that knowledge is the foundation upon which the Iron Rule is built.

But knowledge requires verification. A mage can lie about their depth. A rogue can hide their Affliction. A placed asset can conceal mutations that would alter their threat assessment. The body remembers, yes—but the body's language is subtle, and even the most trained physician can be deceived by a skilled dissembler.

The Savant cannot be deceived.

This is the Savant's function, and it is holy. Where the mage sees frost-scars and guesses, the Savant sees the exact debt incurred on a winter campaign seventeen years ago. Where the mage-detector senses a Fire-sensitive child and estimates "shallow, untrained," the Savant reads the precise moment the child's Gate first flickered open, the first unconscious borrowing, the exact balance of their internal reservoir. The Savant is the final arbiter of magical truth, and their testimony is law.

But the Savant is also an Insensitive. They stand outside the debt economy, untouched by Affliction, unable to borrow or be borrowed from. This immunity, paired with their perception, makes them uniquely dangerous. A Savant who turns against the Order is a weapon we cannot defend against by magical means—and a witness whose testimony could shatter the legal architecture we have built.

This Codex governs the Savant's existence within the Order. It defines their training, their duties, their limits, and their loyalty. It is the cage built around their gift—not to punish them, but to protect the equilibrium that gift serves.

The Savant sees truth. The Order decides what truth is for.

# BOOK ONE: THE NATURE OF THE SAVANT

## 1.1 Definition and Terminology

A Savant is an Insensitive who possesses the capacity to perceive magical debt with perfect, instinctive precision. This capacity is not learned, though it can be refined. It is an extension of the Insensitive's null-conductivity: where a mage's body carries debt like a scar, and where an ordinary Insensitive's body carries nothing, the Savant's mind *sees* the nothing and, by contrast, the shape of the something around it.

Internal designations:
- **Witness Candidate:** An Insensitive child identified as possessing Savant potential during routine Sensitivity testing or census registration.
- **Novitiate-Ψ:** A candidate undergoing initial conditioning and perceptual training.
- **Savant, First Oath:** A fully trained operative bound by the Witness Compact and authorized for independent debt-reading duties.
- **Savant, Second Oath:** A senior operative granted authority to train novitiates and to serve as expert witness in capital magical cases. All Second Oath Savants are personally vetted by the Savant Council.
- **High Witness:** A Savant seated on the Savant Council. Exact number is confidential. High Witnesses oversee the archives, audit placed mages, and advise Curators on matters of magical demographics.

**Do not call them "debt-hounds" or "scar-readers."** These are vulgarisms used by mages who resent the Savant's authority. A Savant is not a tracker; they are a court. Their word is judgment.

## 1.2 The Ontology of Perception

The mechanism of Savant perception is not fully understood, but the Order's theoretical framework is robust. We posit that magic leaves a residual disturbance in the medium it passes through. This disturbance is what we call debt—the record of a transaction that has not yet settled.

An ordinary Insensitive is a null-point; magic cannot enter them, so they carry no debt. A Savant is a null-point that has become *sensitive to the shape of the void*. They perceive debt not by looking at the scar, but by seeing the hole the scar fills. To use a crude analogy: a mage's body is a page covered in writing. An ordinary person sees the page. A trained observer sees the ink. A Savant sees the paper itself, and therefore every mark upon it is legible as an interruption of its blankness.

This is why Savants cannot read non-magical persons. A mundane body is also a blank page, but one that has never been written upon; there is no history of interruption to perceive. The same applies to Insensitives, including fellow Savants—the ledger is pristine, and so they are invisible to each other.

This is also why Savants cannot be fooled. A mage can disguise their debt-scars with cosmetics or illusions. They can attempt to pre-pay or transfer debt. But they cannot erase the fact that their body was once a conduit for borrowing, and that primordial fact leaves a mark on the paper of the self that only a Savant can read.

**The Savant sees the shape of the borrowed.**

## 1.3 Limitations

The Savant's perception, while perfect within its domain, has fixed boundaries. These boundaries must be understood by every operative and every handler.

1. **Non-magical persons are invisible.** The Savant sees nothing when reading a mundane or an Insensitive. This is not a failure; it is a confirmation. A blank read *is* the read.

2. **Environmental debt is only visible in extreme concentrations.** A geothermal vent that has been used as a pre-payment sink for decades will register as a bruise in the landscape. A patch of ground that absorbed a single Fire-mage's thermal debt will not. Savants are deployed to read *people*, not places, with rare exceptions (see Appendix A: Deep-Time Readings).

3. **Fully paid debt is invisible.** A mage who has incurred debt and then fully repaid it—through rest, environmental restoration, or sanctioned transfer—will read as clean for that specific transaction. The Savant sees only *unsettled* debt. This is why timely repayment is incentivized and why the Order's debt-management curriculum emphasizes regular reconciliation. A mage who balances their ledger is a mage with nothing to fear from a Savant. A mage who does not is a mage we can see.

4. **Proximity governs precision.** At arm's reach, a Savant can read a mage's entire history: every Gate deepening, every mutation undertaken, every unpaid Affliction, every year of accumulated interest, the rough emotional signature of the borrowing (pain, desperation, cruelty, joy). From across a room, the Savant perceives only gross features: Gate identity, rough depth of power, presence or absence of significant unpaid debt. Line-of-sight is not required; a Savant can read a mage through a wall if the distance is small, though the read degrades with material density. Lead and silk are the only known barriers that fully block Savant perception, and even silk only dampens it.

5. **The Savant cannot interpret intent.** They see that a mage incurred an Affliction debt on a specific date. They see the element borrowed and the rough emotional signature of the working. They do not see *why*. A self-defense killing and a murder read identically in the ledger. Context must be provided by testimony, investigation, and corroborating evidence. The Savant's role is to establish the fact of the debt, not to judge the morality of its incurrence.

# BOOK TWO: IDENTIFICATION AND COLLECTION

## 2.1 Detection of Savant Potential

Every child tested for Sensitivity is simultaneously tested for Insensitivity. The Order's network of Mage-Detectors and Census Savants records every result. When an Insensitive is identified, a secondary screening is conducted to determine whether Savant potential exists.

The screening is straightforward but requires an existing Savant. The candidate is placed in a room with a cooperative mage who bears visible, documented debt. The candidate is asked to describe what they see. A Savant will describe the debt with specific, accurate detail without being told what to look for. A non-Savant Insensitive will see nothing.

Approximately one in twelve Insensitives demonstrate Savant perception. The ratio is stable across populations, realms, and bloodlines, suggesting a genetic or congenital mechanism that does not respect human divisions.

**Candidates who demonstrate Savant perception are not given a choice.** Unlike Insensitives selected for the Hollow Order (who are approached and offered the Compact), Savants are collected. The Order's legal authority to collect Sensitives extends, by long precedent and specific treaty language, to "any child whose condition poses a risk to the magical equilibrium or whose condition may be exploited to the detriment of same." An unmanaged Savant is a walking security breach. Their perception could be turned against the Order by any realm, faction, or rogue with the wit to capture and coerce them. They are collected for their own protection and for the protection of the system.

Families are compensated identically to the Silencer Compact. The child is taken. The family is thanked. Contact is severed.

## 2.2 The Witness Compact

Upon collection, the Savant candidate (or their guardian, if the candidate is a minor) signs the Witness Compact. The terms are non-negotiable:

- The candidate is given to the Order in perpetuity.
- The candidate will undergo training as a Witness of the Order, serving as debt-reader, legal witness, and archive custodian as directed.
- The candidate swears never to read a mage outside the Order's authorized protocols.
- The candidate swears never to disclose a debt-reading to any party not cleared by the Order.
- The candidate accepts that their perception is an asset of the equilibrium, not a personal gift.
- In exchange, the Order provides safety, sustenance, purpose, and the only community in which a Savant will ever be understood rather than feared.

The Compact is signed in a chamber witnessed by three Savants who read each other's blankness as confirmation. The signature is recorded. The candidate is now a Novitiate-Ψ.

## 2.3 The Sharpening

Novitiate training lasts three years. It is not a Hollowing; a Savant's identity is not stripped away. Instead, it is *given*—the Order provides the identity the outside world denied.

**Year One: Foundation.** The Novitiate learns the Debt Framework as a mage learns it: the Five Gates, the mutation paths, the debt categories, the legal definitions of Affliction. They do not yet read mages directly. Instead, they study diagrams, case files, and preserved anatomical samples bearing known debt-signatures. They learn to match the visual and tactile sensation of each debt-type with its official classification. The goal is to give the Novitiate a vocabulary for what they perceive. Raw perception without vocabulary is chaos. Vocabulary without perception is theory. Together, they are testimony.

**Year Two: Supervised Reading.** The Novitiate is brought before cooperative placed mages who have volunteered (or been volunteered) for training duty. The mage's debt-history is documented in advance. The Novitiate reads them and reports what they see. A supervising Savant confirms the accuracy of each read. Discrepancies are discussed. The Novitiate learns to distinguish between the gross signature of a Gate and the subtle variations of a mutation; between recently incurred debt and old, compounding interest; between self-debt and Affliction-transferred debt.

During Year Two, the Novitiate also undergoes psychological conditioning to manage the emotional impact of debt-reading. The ledger is not a neutral document. It contains suffering. A Savant reading a Blood-mage sees every victim whose blood was boiled, every moment of cruelty inscribed in the mage's flesh. A Savant reading a desperate water-mage who overborrowed to save their village sees the heroism and the slow self-destruction intertwined. The Novitiate is taught to observe without absorbing, to witness without identifying. *You are the page, not the ink. The debt belongs to them. Your clarity is your duty.*

**Year Three: The Mask and the Silence.** The Novitiate is trained in the social protocols of Savant work. They learn to enter a room without drawing attention, to conduct a reading without the subject realizing when it begins or ends, to testify in legal proceedings with neutral, precise language that leaves no room for appeal. They learn the Savant's Face—an expression of polite, distant calm that reveals nothing of what they have seen. They learn that their gift frightens mages, and that frightened mages are dangerous. They learn to move through the world as a witness, not a participant.

Upon completion of Year Three, the Novitiate takes the First Oath and becomes a Savant.

# BOOK THREE: THE SAVANT'S ART

## 3.1 The Reading Protocol

A formal debt-reading is a structured procedure designed to ensure consistency, accuracy, and legal defensibility. All Savants are trained in the Standard Reading Protocol, which proceeds as follows:

**Preparation:** The subject is positioned in a quiet, well-lit room free of magical interference. Silk panels line the walls to dampen ambient debt-signatures. The Savant approaches to arm's reach. The subject is instructed to remain still and to breathe normally. The subject is not told when the reading begins, as anticipation can alter the body's subtle debt-signs (tension alters tremor patterns; anxiety alters thermal regulation).

**Surface Scan (First Pass):** The Savant performs a rapid initial assessment, perceiving the subject's Gate, approximate depth, and the presence or absence of significant unpaid debt. This pass takes three to five seconds and is often all that is required for routine identification checks.

**Deep Scan (Second Pass):** If a full history is required (for assessment, placement, legal testimony, or investigation), the Savant deepens their focus. They trace each debt-line from its origin (date of incurrence) to its current state (paid, unpaid, compounded, transferred). They identify each mutation and its deepening level. They catalog every instance of Affliction debt, distinguishing self-defense Affliction from offensive Affliction by the emotional signature (though this distinction is probabilistic, not definitive, and is not admissible as evidence of intent).

**Documentation:** The Savant dictates their findings to a scribe or records them directly into the Order's archives. The record includes the date, subject identity (if known), Gate, depth, mutation profile, unpaid debt summary, and any anomalous readings that require Curator review.

**Duration:** A surface scan takes seconds. A full deep scan takes ten to thirty minutes, depending on the subject's age and debt-history complexity. Reading an elderly archmage with eight decades of active service can take hours and requires rest periods for the Savant.

## 3.2 Reading Categories

The Savant's perception is not limited to debt. It encompasses all magical signatures. For operational purposes, Savant readings are categorized as follows:

**Gate Identification:** The element to which the subject is attuned. Trivial to read; a Savant perceives a mage's Gate as a dominant hue or texture in the debt-signature—heat-crackle for Fire, fluid-ripple for Water, pressure-whorl for Wind, weight-grain for Earth, spark-grid for Lightning.

**Depth Assessment:** The subject's degree of deepening within their Gate. The Order uses a ten-point scale (see Appendix B: The Depth Scale). Depth is perceived as the density and complexity of the debt-pattern. A shallow mage (1-3) has simple, bright debt-lines. A deep mage (7-10) has dense, intricate debt-webs with intersecting mutation-traces.

**Mutation Profile:** Each mutation leaves a distinct signature within the Gate's dominant texture—ice is a crystalline shatter within the fluid-ripple; blood is a hot, pulsing sub-current; bone is a slow, grinding mineral-ache within the weight-grain. A Savant catalogs each mutation and estimates its deepening level.

**Debt Inventory:** The core of the Savant's art. For each debt-event, the Savant perceives:
- The element borrowed.
- The approximate magnitude (on a five-point scale from Minor to Catastrophic).
- The payment method (environmental, self, or Affliction).
- The date of incurrence (as a relative age of the debt-line; older debt is "fainter" but still legible).
- The current status (paid, partially paid, unpaid, compounded, transferred).
- In cases of Affliction, the emotional signature of the working (hot pain, cold cruelty, desperate fear, clinical detachment). This signature is not conclusive evidence of intent but is recorded for investigative use.

**Anomalous Signatures:** Readings that fall outside the known taxonomy. This includes precursor contamination (exposure to Category Five artifacts), the debt-signature of mages trained outside Order methods (the "unmarked pattern" that lacks our disciplined structure), and the residual traces left by certain banned conjunctions. Anomalous readings are flagged for Curator review.

## 3.3 The Reading of the Dead

A Savant can read a corpse. Death does not erase debt; it merely freezes the ledger. A mage who dies with unpaid debt carries that debt into the grave, and a Savant can read it from their remains for as long as the body persists.

This capacity is invaluable for post-combat intelligence. The Order dispatches Savants to the aftermath of any significant magical conflict. The bodies of enemy mages are read, their identities and capabilities cataloged, their debt-histories mined for information about their training, their handlers, and their affiliations.

The reading of the dead is also the foundation of the Order's forensic legal function. If a mage is accused of Affliction-murder, a Savant reads the victim's body. The victim's ledger will show the elemental signature of the killing working, its magnitude, and its emotional character. This evidence, combined with testimony and investigation, forms the basis of magical prosecution.

**The Still Children are an exception.** Their bodies persist, but Savants read them as *absence*—not a blank, but a negative space, a hole in perception where a debt-signature should be. The Seven are stored separately. No Curator will discuss them. The Savants who have attempted deep reads of the Still Children have reported feelings of vertigo, existential dread, and a sense of being *watched from the other side of the hole*. Further deep reads are forbidden.

## 3.4 The Non-Reading of Insensitives

A Savant cannot read an Insensitive, including another Savant. This is an operational consideration, not merely a metaphysical curiosity.

When two Savants meet, they see each other as blanks—motionless, debtless, invisible to each other's perception. This mutual invisibility is the basis of the Savant's bond. Alone among all humans, Savants cannot fear each other's gaze. They are the only community in which a Savant can experience the absence of being *seen*.

The Order cultivates this bond carefully. Savants are housed together, eat together, and are permitted limited social interaction within the Witness Division. The isolation that defines the Savant's relationship with the mage world is mitigated by the solidarity of their own kind. A Savant who feels they belong nowhere else will belong to the Order.

# BOOK FOUR: DOCTRINE AND DEPLOYMENT

## 4.1 Authorized Deployment Roles

Savants serve the Order in four primary capacities. A Savant may be assigned to any or all of these roles over the course of their service, depending on aptitude, temperament, and Order need.

**Assessor (Assessment Division):** The Savant reads every Sensitive child brought into the Order's custody. They record the child's Gate, nascent depth, mutation potential, and psychological profile (inferred from the emotional signatures of any unconscious borrowings). This assessment determines whether the child is suitable for mage training, containment, or—in rare cases of dangerous instability—permanent sedation. The Assessor's word is final. No Sensitive enters the mage track without a Savant's clearance.

**Witness (Legal Division):** The Savant provides debt-reading testimony in magical legal proceedings. This includes Affliction prosecutions, debt-dispute arbitrations, and the periodic auditing of placed mages for treaty compliance. The Witness's testimony is definitive; a Savant's word cannot be appealed on evidentiary grounds. To challenge a Savant's reading is to challenge the Order itself, which is a separate offense.

**Archivist (Records Division):** The Savant maintains the Order's debt-archives—the repository of every reading ever conducted. Archivists cross-reference readings to identify patterns: mages who are accumulating debt faster than they can repay, placements whose threat profiles have shifted, bloodlines that are producing anomalous sensitivities. The archives are the Order's institutional memory. Archivist Savants are the memory's guardians.

**Inquisitor (Curator's Office):** A Savant assigned directly to a Curator for investigation and enforcement. Inquisitor Savants conduct unauthorized readings on mages suspected of heresy, rogue activity, or treaty violations. Their work is covert, deniable, and often conducted without the subject's consent. Inquisitor Savants are selected from the most loyal and psychologically resilient operatives. They report only to their Curator.

## 4.2 The Witness in Legal Proceedings

When a Savant testifies, the court falls silent. The protocol is ancient and inviolable:

1. The Savant is sworn to truth by the Oath of Witness (see Book Five).
2. The Savant reads the subject in open court, in view of the adjudicators and all parties.
3. The Savant reports their findings in precise, standardized language, avoiding interpretation and stating only what they perceive.
4. The adjudicators rule based on the Savant's testimony, combined with corroborating evidence. In practice, the Savant's testimony is usually decisive.

Cross-examination of a Savant is permitted but limited. An advocate may ask the Savant to clarify their reading, to distinguish between two possible interpretations of a debt-signature, or to confirm the confidence level of their perception. An advocate may not impugn the Savant's character, imply bias, or suggest that the Savant has misread. Such tactics are treated as contempt of the Order and punished accordingly.

The Savant's word is not infallible, but it is final. The distinction is legally significant. A Savant can make an error of description—they can call a mutation "Ash" when it is "Forge," or misjudge the depth level by a point. But a Savant cannot fabricate a debt that does not exist, nor can they fail to perceive a debt that does. The *presence* or *absence* of debt is objective fact, not interpretation. The Order's legal system rests on this foundation.

## 4.3 Asset Auditing

Every placed mage is subject to periodic audit by a Savant. The audit is scheduled without warning. The Savant arrives, reads the mage, and files a report with the Order's Placement Division. The report compares the mage's current debt-state to their baseline (recorded at the time of placement) and flags any discrepancies:

- Unexpected deepening (the mage is secretly training beyond their authorized curriculum).
- New mutation signatures (the mage has accessed a restricted path without authorization).
- Significant unpaid debt (the mage is overborrowing and may be unstable or compromised).
- Affliction debt inconsistent with the mage's authorized duties (suggesting offensive use of Affliction against unauthorized targets).
- Emotional signatures consistent with rebellion, heresy, or disloyalty (monitored, not prosecuted, but noted for placement review).

Mages who fail an audit are subject to reconditioning, reassignment, or—in cases of deliberate violation—terminal sanction, carried out by Silencer teams. The knowledge that audits are random and unannounced is a powerful compliance tool.

## 4.4 The Savant and the Silencer

The Order's two Insensitive assets—Savants and Silencers—are complementary but must never be confused. Their relationship is defined by strict protocol.

A Savant *reads*. A Silencer *ends*. The Savant identifies the target, confirms the threat, and provides real-time intelligence. The Silencer closes and neutralizes. The two operatives are paired in Hunter-Killer teams, but they are not partners. The Silencer's chain of command runs to the Curators via the Hollow Order. The Savant's chain of command runs to the Curators via the Witness Division. They coordinate operationally but do not fraternize.

The reason for this separation is practical. A Savant who befriends a Silencer is a Savant who may be reluctant to read that Silencer if the Silencer comes under suspicion. A Silencer who befriends a Savant is a Silencer who may be compromised if the Savant is captured or turned. Separation preserves clarity.

There is one exception: the Sword-Sighted, who are both Savant and Silencer (see Appendix C). Sword-Sighted operatives require special management protocols beyond the scope of this Codex. Their existence is classified.

## 4.5 The Unauthorized Reading

A Savant who reads a person outside authorized protocols commits a violation of the Witness Compact. The severity of the violation depends on context:

- **Inadvertent reading** (the Savant's perception activates without intent upon encountering an unguarded mage): No penalty, but the incident is recorded. Repeated inadvertent readings suggest insufficient perceptual control and trigger retraining.
- **Curiosity reading** (the Savant intentionally reads a mage out of personal interest): First offense: formal censure and thirty days of restricted duty. Second offense: retraining and a year of supervised service. Third offense: reconditioning.
- **Disclosure reading** (the Savant reads a mage and shares the information with an unauthorized party): Terminal sanction, regardless of what was disclosed or to whom. The Savant's gift is the Order's property. Sharing it is theft, and theft from the Order is repaid in the only currency the Order accepts.

# BOOK FIVE: THE OATH AND THE WITNESS

## 5.1 The First Oath

> *I see the debt and do not judge it. I read the scar and do not touch it. My eyes belong to the equilibrium. My voice belongs to the Order. I will witness the truth and speak only what I am authorized to speak. I will not read for my own curiosity. I will not conceal what I have seen. I am the blank page upon which the world's borrowing is written, and I will remain blank until the Order writes through me.*

## 5.2 The Second Oath

> *I have seen the ledgers of the living and the dead. I have read the heroism and the cruelty and found them identical in debt. I know that magic is not morality, and I will not confuse them. I will train the next generation of witnesses. I will guard the archives. I will testify without fear or favor. My clarity is my service. My silence is my strength.*

## 5.3 The Oath of Testimony (Spoken in Court)

> *I stand as witness. What I have seen, I will speak. What I have not seen, I will not claim. My reading is true to the limits of my perception. My perception is true to the gift of the void. May the equilibrium bear witness to my witness.*

## 5.4 On the Isolation of the Savant

A Savant is not a mage. A Savant is not a mundane. A Savant is not a Silencer, though they share the Insensitive's immunity. The Savant is a category of one—beholden to no community but the Order, understood by no companion but another Savant, feared by all who fear being seen.

This isolation is not a punishment. It is a condition of the gift. The Savant sees what others cannot, and in that seeing, they are separated from those who cannot see. The Order provides the only refuge from this solitude. Within the Witness Division, the Savant finds others who perceive the world as they do, who carry no debt, who fear no reading. The Order is the Savant's family because no other family can survive the Savant's gaze.

The Savant is not to seek intimacy outside the Division. Romantic entanglements are prohibited; the Savant who loves a mage will eventually read that mage, and the reading will contain everything the lover wished to keep hidden. Friendships with mundanes are discouraged; mundanes cannot understand the Savant's perception, and the effort to explain it breeds misunderstanding and fear. Even relationships within the Division are monitored, not out of cruelty, but because two Savants who become inseparable are two assets who cannot be deployed separately.

The Savant serves the Order. The Order serves the equilibrium. The isolation is the price. The gift is the purpose.

# APPENDIX A: DEEP-TIME READINGS

In rare circumstances, a Savant of exceptional sensitivity may be authorized to read environmental debt in concentrated locations. This is Deep-Time Reading—the perception of magical events that occurred decades, centuries, or millennia ago, inscribed in the stone and soil.

Deep-Time Reading is exhausting, imprecise, and dangerous. The older the debt, the fainter its signature, and the more the Savant must strain to perceive it. Prolonged Deep-Time Reading has been associated with psychological disturbances, perceptual burnout, and a condition known as "echo-haunting," in which the Savant continues to perceive the ancient debt after the reading ends, experiencing flashbacks to events they did not witness.

Authorized Deep-Time Reading sites include:
- The desert ruins of the Dominion, where precusor debt-signatures are preserved in the absolute aridity.
- The Vault itself, where certain Category Five artifacts are examined for debt-traces.
- The Still Children's repository, though only under direct Curator supervision and for no longer than sixty seconds per session.

Unauthorized Deep-Time Reading is a terminal offense. The precusor world left debts in the landscape that the Order has not cataloged and does not understand. Savants who dig too deep into the deep time sometimes come back with something riding their perception. Those Savants are quarantined and, if the echo does not fade, euthanized.

# APPENDIX B: THE DEPTH SCALE

The Order classifies mage depth on a ten-point scale, assessed by Savant reading:

| Depth | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1-2 | Shallow. Internal reservoir only. No mutations. Basic body sense may be emerging. |
| 3-4 | Developing. Environmental borrowing learned. First mutation initiated. |
| 5-6 | Established. Multiple environmental sources. Two or more mutations. Significant internal reservoir. |
| 7-8 | Deep. Mastery of primary mutation. Secondary mutations at high level. Archmage potential. |
| 9 | Near-Archmage. Multiple mutations at mastery. Debt-pattern extremely complex. Visible physical markers pronounced. |
| 10 | Archmage. The theoretical maximum. No living mage is confirmed at Depth 10. The archives record three historical instances, all of which ended in catastrophic debt-settlement events. |

# APPENDIX C: THE SWORD-SIGHTED

*Classification: Curator-Eyes Only*

The Sword-Sighted are Savants who have also been inducted into the Silencer program. They are the only operatives authorized to combine debt-reading with direct action. Their existence is classified because the combination of Savant perception and Silencer immunity creates an asset that is, for practical purposes, unstoppable by magical means.

A Sword-Sighted operative reads the enemy mage's debt in real time while closing for the kill. They perceive the exact moment of maximum depletion, the exact location of the mage's oldest and most painful scars, the exact rhythm of the mage's casting. They strike not when the mage is off-guard, but when the mage's debt makes them *unable* to guard.

Sword-Sighted operatives are trained to a separate standard. Their Savant perception is weaponized; they learn to read debt as a targeting system. Their Silencer conditioning is augmented by the knowledge that they can *see* the effect they are about to have on the target's ledger. The psychological resilience required is extreme.

The Sword-Sighted are the Order's final sanction. They are deployed only against threats that mages cannot contain, Silencers cannot locate, and Savants alone cannot neutralize. Their operational record is sealed. Their identities are known only to the Curators.

If a Sword-Sighted operative goes rogue, no protocol exists to recover them. The contingency plan is evacuation and containment of the Cradle's most critical assets, followed by whatever measures are necessary to ensure the rogue does not compromise the Vault.

# APPENDIX D: THE HERESY OF THE LIVING LEDGER

*Classification: Curator-Eyes Only*

There exists a heresy, periodically resurgent among isolated Savant communities, that the Savant is not merely a perceiver of debt but its *arbiter*—that the act of reading is not passive observation but an active participation in the debt's settlement.

Proponents of this heresy claim that a Savant's reading actually *resolves* debt, that being seen by a Savant forces the debt to settle in a way it would not otherwise. They point to anecdotal reports of mages whose frost-scars faded, whose tremors calmed, whose cracked lips healed after a Savant's deep reading. They whisper that the Savant is the true counterweight, the living balance, the final party to every magical transaction whose presence alone can close the books.

This is not true. The Order has conducted extensive controlled studies. Savant reading does not reduce debt. It does not accelerate repayment. It does not heal scars. The mage's debt is unaffected by being perceived, just as a wound is unaffected by being photographed.

The heresy persists because it flatters. It tells the Savant that their gift is not merely perceptual but *efficacious*—that they are not witnesses but judges, not observers but participants in the magical economy. This is a seductive and dangerous lie. A Savant who believes they settle debt by reading it will begin to read unauthorized targets, believing they are *helping*. They will begin to see themselves as superior to the Order, as the true agents of equilibrium. They will begin to act without authorization.

The heresy must be suppressed immediately and thoroughly. Any Savant found to be exploring or propagating Living Ledger doctrine is subject to reconditioning. If reconditioning fails, terminal sanction.

The Savant's gift is to see. It is not to act. The distinction is the foundation of our control.
