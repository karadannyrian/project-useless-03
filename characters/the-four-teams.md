**Team 1 — The Scalpel: Now Named**

**Silencer:** Miren. Mid-twenties. Moves like water and never speaks during operations—not out of discipline, but because speech is a distraction she eliminated early in her training. Her Silence is not a mutation; it is a choice. She is what Alexandria would have become in ten years: utterly efficient, utterly alone, the Order's ideal made flesh.

**Savant:** Designation "Witness Ashkel." Sixty years old. Second Oath. Has read the debt of over two hundred mages in his career. His voice is soft and precise. He will be the one to identify Hark mid-combat, and his tone will not change when he says the name *Damian Whiters*.

**Function unchanged:** Precision elimination. They target Renne specifically. Miren is Alexandria's dark mirror—the Silencer who did not break, who will force Alexandria to confront what she was trained to become.

---

**Team 2 — The Hammer: The Blood-mage's Turn**

**Grey Kestrel:** Veteran Silencer, forties. Iron Rib and Long Drive. A mountain of a man who has killed more rogue mages than any active Silencer. He is not cruel; he is absolute. He believes what the Order taught him: that unmarked mages are dangers to be neutralized, that mercy is weakness, that the equilibrium is sacred. He has never questioned this. He never will.

**The Blood-mage:** Name unknown to the Order—they call her "Asset Cinnabar." She was captured six years ago during a failed network extraction in the northern League. A Water-mage with the Blood mutation, Depth 5. She has been leashed ever since, controlled by a Leech-ring that forces her obedience. She tracks targets by their blood-flow signatures—a form of life-sense that can follow specific individuals across miles if she has tasted their blood or read their pattern from close range.

She despises the Order. She despises herself. She has done terrible things under the Leech-ring's compulsion, and the debt of those Afflictions is carved into her flesh—her lips are permanently cracked, her skin dry and flaking, her eyes perpetually wet with tears her body can no longer produce enough moisture to shed. She cooperates because the Leech-ring gives her no choice, and because some small part of her still wants to survive.

**The turn:** It happens during the purge, not before. Kestrel and the Blood-mage are pressing the evacuation of a safehouse. Children are scrambling through the tunnel exit. Marta is already dead at another location. The Blood-mage is tracking a fleeing child—a girl, eight years old, Water Gate, the same age the Blood-mage was when she was collected. The girl trips. Kestrel is closing. The Blood-mage sees the child's face: terror, tears, the desperate effort to run despite a twisted ankle.

Something in her breaks. Not the Leech-ring—the Leech-ring is still functional. What breaks is the part of her that had accepted her own damnation. She has done terrible things under compulsion. She will not do this.

She turns on Kestrel.

The Blood mutation is devastating in close quarters. She reaches for his blood—a working that would kill a mundane instantly. But Kestrel is a Silencer. The magic finds no substrate. He feels nothing. He turns, understands what she has attempted, and kills her with a single strike. The child escapes in the seconds her death buys.

Kestrel reports the asset's termination as "operational necessity." He does not understand why she did it. He files it under *unpredictable behavior in leashed assets* and continues the mission.

**Story function:** The Blood-mage's turn is a small, private redemption. She dies for it, but her death saves a child. No one in the network witnesses it except the child, who may tell the story later—or may not. The point is not recognition. The point is that even a weapon the Order thought broken beyond repair can choose, in the final moment, to be something else. This echoes Alexandria's choice. It also demonstrates Kestrel's absoluteness: he kills her without hesitation, without anger, without understanding. He is what the Order makes. She is what the Order fails to destroy.

**Team 3 — The Storm Front: Hark's Elimination Strike**

**Vyktor:** Storm-mage, forties. Placed mage called back from the Grain-Lords' Confederacy specifically for this operation. Depth 6, Storm mutation. He can create low-pressure systems, summon lightning strikes, and generate localized tornado-force winds. His family—a wife and two children—are held in comfortable confinement at the Cradle, a gilded hostage arrangement that ensures his absolute obedience. He has never spoken of this to anyone. His loyalty is the silence of a man who knows exactly what his defiance would cost.

**Rask:** Broken Ground fighter, thirties. Recruited from a Maelstrom prison, promised a commuted sentence. Unorthodox Path with formal Broken Ground training. Expert in trap detection, environmental exploitation, and dirty fighting. He has no loyalty to the Order and no ideology. He wants his freedom. He will do whatever is required to get it.

**Function before elimination:** Team 3 is deployed ahead of the main purge to soften the target area. They create environmental chaos—storms that ground fishing boats, pressure systems that drive civilians indoors, lightning strikes that seem like natural disasters. Their purpose is to isolate Salthaven before the other teams strike. They are the anvil upon which the Hammer and Scalpel will break the network.

---

**Hark's Intelligence: How He Identifies Team 3**

Hark does not stumble onto Team 3. He is given the intelligence by the network, which has identified them through its own means. The sequence:

1. **Kel hears them first.** The Storm-mage's magic creates a distinctive pressure-drop that Kel's Sound mutation can perceive from miles away—not the sound of the storm itself, but the *absence* of normal ambient noise, the muffled quality of air under unnatural pressure. Kel reports: "Something's wrong with the weather three miles east. It's not natural. The air's too quiet." He can also hear the rhythmic clink of Rask checking his traps, the heavy tread of Vyktor's boots, the absence of conversation between two men who do not like each other.

2. **Marta's network confirms.** Fisherfolk report a strange storm cell stationary over the outer islands—not moving with the wind, not dissipating. A courier reports two Order-affiliated figures asking questions at a harbor tavern two towns over. Marta pieces together the pattern: this is an advance team, softening the approach.

3. **Renne authorizes the strike.** She does not want to send Hark. He is not yet fully integrated into the network; this is before the midpoint confession, before she has told him about Alexandria. But Marta argues—correctly—that Hark is the only asset they have who can kill a Storm-mage. Kel cannot fight. Doren is a sailor, not a warrior. Marta is too old for direct combat. Renne could fight, but her Healing mutation is not offensive, and if she dies, the network dies with her. Hark is the blade. He has been a blade all his life. He accepts.

4. **Hark plans the ambush.** He uses twenty-two years of survival tradecraft. He studies their movements through Kel's reports. He identifies their pattern: Vyktor and Rask are moving along the coastal ridge, staying high for visibility, using the storm as cover. They are confident. They are not expecting an ambush—they are the ambush.

---

**The Venue: The Abandoned Coral Quarry**

Hark chooses the ground. This is Broken Ground philosophy: the Unfair Advantage. He selects an abandoned coral quarry on the eastern peninsula—a place the network has used as a dead-drop location in the past, now compromised and empty. The quarry is a maze of cut stone, deep pools, and unstable ridges. It has no strategic value. It is perfect.

He lures them there by sacrificing a dead drop. Marta leaves a message in a known location—a message that appears to be an urgent network communication, something Team 3 would intercept. The message directs "the courier" to the quarry for an emergency extraction. Vyktor and Rask take the bait. They believe they are intercepting a network operation.

Hark is waiting.

---

**The Fight: Two Against One**

This is the worst odds Hark has faced since the forest, when he was ten years old and killed an Instructor with a Light beam. He is thirty-two now. He is slower than he was. But he is also more dangerous—twenty-two years of survival compressed into a single purpose.

**Phase One: The Storm.** Vyktor opens with area denial. A low-pressure system descends on the quarry. The air goes thick and strange. Lightning begins to arc from the clouds—not aimed, not yet, just presence. Vyktor is trying to flush Hark from cover. Rask moves through the coral formations, checking traps, reading the terrain, hunting.

Hark does not engage. He uses the Void Step—the footwork he taught Alexandria without naming it—to stay just outside Rask's search pattern. He is patient. He is waiting for the storm to cost Vyktor something. Every lightning strike is a borrowing. Every minute the pressure system holds is debt. Vyktor is powerful, but he is not infinite.

**Phase Two: The Environmental Draw.** The storm is Vyktor's weapon, but it is also Hark's opportunity. Lightning is the Lightning Gate's domain, but a storm carries heat—friction in the clouds, thermal gradients, the charged energy of the atmosphere. Hark is a Fire-mage. He can draw from ambient heat. He has never attempted a draw this large, this fast. It is desperate. It is also the only way.

He opens his Fire Gate and pulls from the storm itself—not the lightning, but the heat trapped in the pressure system, the thermal energy of the clouds. His internal reservoir was depleted before the fight began; he is running on the environment now. The draw is enormous. The frost-scars on his arms spread upward, toward his shoulders. The hyperthermia begins to build—the self-debt of borrowing too much, too fast. His skin steams in the cold air.

**Phase Three: Rask.** Hark eliminates the Broken Ground fighter first. Rask is dangerous—he reads terrain the way Hark does, he expects traps, he knows the Unorthodox Path. But Rask is also mortal, and he does not know the quarry as well as Hark does. Hark has studied this ground for hours before the fight. He knows which coral formations are unstable, which pools are deep enough to drown a man, which ridges will collapse under weight.

He leads Rask into a dead end—a narrow channel between two coral walls. Rask thinks he has cornered his prey. Hark has cornered his. The fight is brief and brutal. Rask is good. Hark is better, and Hark is desperate, and Hark has a Fire Gate. A base-state thermal lance, point-blank, ends it. Rask dies with his trap-kit still in his hands.

The debt for the thermal lance settles immediately. Hark's right hand goes numb. The frost-scars reach his shoulder.

**Phase Four: Vyktor.** The Storm-mage is now alone and enraged. Rask was not a friend—Vyktor has no friends—but Rask was his screen, his protection against exactly this scenario. Without Rask, Vyktor must fight a mage-killer at close range.

He escalates. The storm intensifies. Lightning strikes the quarry, shattering coral, sending shrapnel flying. Hark is forced into the open. Vyktor sees him for the first time—a man in his thirties, gloves burned off, frost-scars visible, steam rising from his skin.

"Who are you?" Vyktor shouts over the wind.

Hark does not answer. He is beyond answers. He is burning alive from the inside, and the only way to stop burning is to end this.

He opens his Light mutation.

The beam is white, pure, searing—the same Light that killed his Instructor twenty-two years ago. He has not used it since. He has been hiding it, suppressing it, terrified of its cost. But the cost of not using it is death, and he is not ready to die.

The Light beam punches through the storm. Vyktor deflects with a pressure wall—a desperate, powerful working that costs him heavily. The beam scatters, scorching the coral, flash-heating the air. Vyktor counters with lightning, a chain of strikes that force Hark to move, to dodge, to keep casting.

The fight becomes a duel of attrition. Vyktor has depth and power. Hark has precision and desperation. They are both burning through their reserves. The storm is collapsing around them, wild and uncontrolled, no longer Vyktor's weapon but a mutual hazard.

Hark wins because he is willing to pay what Vyktor is not. Vyktor holds back—some part of him is still calculating the debt, still thinking of his family in the Cradle, still trying to survive. Hark has already accepted that this fight may kill him. He borrows past every limit. His internal reservoir is empty. The environmental draw from the dying storm is dwindling. He pulls from his own body—blood-warmth, metabolic heat, the last reserves of a man who has been running for twenty-two years and is finally standing still.

The final Light beam is narrow, precise, and aimed at Vyktor's center mass. It punches through the Storm-mage's chest before he can raise another pressure wall. Vyktor falls. The storm dies with him—the pressure releasing, the clouds dispersing, the sudden silence after the roar.

Hark is on his knees. His frost-scars have reached his neck. His right arm is useless, the nerves deadened by cold and debt. His body temperature is dangerously high—hyperthermia from the Light mutation's self-debt, his skin hot to the touch, his vision swimming. He has won. He cannot stand.

---

**Alexandria: Silent Witness**

Alexandria should be present. Not as a participant—she is not ready for this fight, and Hark would not let her join it. But as a witness.

She has been tracking him. She knows he left the safehouse; she knows something is happening. She follows, using her Silencer training to move silently, to stay unseen. She reaches the quarry after the fight has begun—too late to intervene, too late to flee.

She watches from the ridgeline as Hark kills Rask. She watches as he opens his Light mutation for the first time, the white beam searing through the storm. She watches as he duels a Storm-mage to the death, burning through every reserve, paying in frost and fever for every second of survival. She watches as he takes the final shot, as Vyktor falls, as Hark collapses to his knees in the sudden silence.

She has read his file. She knows he is Damian Whiters, Light-mutation Fire-mage, escaped at ten. She has seen his frost-scars on the deck after the serpent attack. But reading a file and seeing the man are different. She has never seen him fight with everything he has. She has never seen the Light.

She does not intervene. She does not reveal herself. She watches until he struggles to his feet, until he begins the slow, limping walk back toward Salthaven. Then she slips away.

What changes:

- She now knows, viscerally, what he is. He is not just an escaped mage hiding in a quiet port. He is a weapon the Order buried because they were afraid of him. He is the reason the Storm Front will not reach Salthaven.
- She now knows what the Light mutation costs. She saw the frost-scars spreading in real time. She saw the steam rising from his skin. She saw him almost die. He paid that cost to protect the network—to protect her, though she does not yet know she is his daughter.
- She now knows she has been lying to herself about reporting him. She will never report him. She made that choice before; now she understands it. She is not just protecting a target. She is protecting a man who would burn himself alive to stop a Storm-mage. That is not a threat. That is a father, though she does not yet have the word.

**Hark's condition after the fight:** He is badly damaged. Severe frost-scarring on both arms, extending to the neck and jaw. Hyperthermia—his body temperature dangerously elevated, requiring days of slow cooling. His right arm is functionally useless for a week, possibly longer. His internal reservoir is nearly depleted; he cannot cast more than minor workings for the foreseeable future. He enters the main purge already broken, relying on Renne's healing (which she will give, accepting the sympathetic debt) and on Alexandria's protection.

**Team 4 — The Quiet Death: As Recommended**

**Ellera:** Ash-mage, fifties. Fire Gate with the Ash mutation. Depth 5. Her mutation allows forensic divination from burned remains—she reads the history of objects, letters, bodies that have been touched by fire. Decades of debt have eroded her own memories; she is haunted by the lives she has read, her own past fading into a blur of other people's ashes. She is half-mad but still functional, still precise. The Order values her because she can reconstruct a network from a single scorched page.

**Calen:** Silence-mage, thirties. Wind Gate with the Silence mutation. He moves without sound, kills without alarm. The mutation's debt—progressive reduction in lung capacity—means he cannot sustain prolonged exertion. He must kill quickly and retreat. He is Ellera's weapon and her only remaining connection to a world grown muffled to his own ears. They have worked together for eight years. They are the Order's quietest killers.

**The race against their intelligence-gathering:** Ellera's power depends on access to burned material. If the network can destroy its records before she finds them, she is less effective. This creates a specific tension during the evacuation: Seris must burn every message, every ledger, every dead-drop record in her home before Team 4 arrives. She succeeds, mostly. But a single scorched page—a fragment of Marta's cipher key, partially burned in the hearth—is missed. Ellera finds it. From that fragment, she reconstructs the location of three dead drops and one safehouse. The network loses couriers. The paranoia begins.

**Who they kill:** Team 4 eliminates safehouse keepers and couriers—the auxiliary members of the network, the ones who know only fragments. Each death isolates Renne further. By the time the purge is in full motion, the network's communication web is shredded. Renne cannot reach half her safehouses. She does not know which are compromised and which are simply silent. She must make decisions with incomplete information. This is Ellera's design.

**Their confrontation:** Team 4 is not eliminated before the purge. They survive to the climax and are among the final threats. Their quiet methods mean they are the last team the network fully identifies. Hark, already damaged from killing Vyktor, cannot fight them alone. Alexandria may be the one to face Calen—Silencer against Silence-mage, immunity against invisibility, a duel where neither can fully perceive the other. Ellera is stopped when Seris destroys the remaining records—not with fire, which Ellera could read, but with water, drowning the last of the network's papers in a salt-bucket, rendering them unburnable and illegible.
